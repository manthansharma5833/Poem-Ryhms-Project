Dreaming of Li Po

Cedar Sigo

II
This cloud of souls has wandered all over the sky,
gone to the corner store and may never come back
I have dreamed of your face for three nights and each time
it rises, it redefines itself
You warn of general dangers and rise quickly to leave
iced over fears, born of an iron ship torn in half
and the silhouette in aggregate placed in a tiny doorway,
his expression a touch bewildered
The city is run by enterprising zombies and occasional artists
I choose to sit in the center of an empty white room as a half dead flower
The net of dream and interdependence gets torn throughout the night
—haphazard curses begin to fly (one thousand years of infamy)
What good is all of this when I am left for dead and looking on?

Death List

Jeddie Sophronius

As many as 5,000 names were furnished over a period of months to the army there, and the Americans later checked off the names of those who had been killed or captured, according to the former U.S. officials… “No one cared, so long as they were communists, that they were being butchered,” said Howard Federspiel, who in 1965 was the Indonesia expert at the State Department’s bureau of intelligence and research. “No one was getting very worked up about it.” —Kathy Kadane, “U.S. Officials’ Lists Aided Indonesian Bloodbath in ’60s”, The Washington Post, May 21, 1990 Lives perished, yet
nothing happened.
Earth’s rotation
did not stop. It
happened elsewhere,
somewhere distant:
not here. It was
contained as in,
the earthquake was
contained: struck a
place much farther
than the distance
the average man
would care. The dead
was not our dead.
The dead were at
fault. The dead were
our enemies,
The dead chose wrong.
They weren’t alive
to begin with. Nothing
happened. No one cared.
