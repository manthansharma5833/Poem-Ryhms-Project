Dreaming of Li Po

Cedar Sigo

II
This cloud of souls has wandered all over the sky,
gone to the corner store and may never come back
I have dreamed of your face for three nights and each time
it rises, it redefines itself
You warn of general dangers and rise quickly to leave
iced over fears, born of an iron ship torn in half
and the silhouette in aggregate placed in a tiny doorway,
his expression a touch bewildered
The city is run by enterprising zombies and occasional artists
I choose to sit in the center of an empty white room as a half dead flower
The net of dream and interdependence gets torn throughout the night
—haphazard curses begin to fly (one thousand years of infamy)
What good is all of this when I am left for dead and looking on?

Cedar Sigo is a poet and member of the Suquamish Nation. He studied at The Jack Kerouac School of Disembodied Poetics at the Naropa Institute. He is the author of endless books and pamphlets of poetry, including All This Time (Wave Books, 2021), Stranger in Town (City Lights, 2010), Expensive Magic (House Press, 2008), two editions of Selected Writings (Ugly Duckling Presse, 2003 and 2005), and most recently Siren of Atlantis (Wave Books, 2025). In 2022 he received a grants to artist’s award from The Foundation for Contemporary Arts. He has taught all over the country, including The University of Washington, Bard College, Washington University, Naropa University, and The Institute of American Indian Arts. He lives in Lofall, Washington.
